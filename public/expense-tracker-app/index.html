Guide

What Is an Expense Tracker App?

An expense tracker app is a digital tool that lets you record, categorise, and analyse your income and spending. Instead of trying to remember where your money went at the end of the month, you log each transaction as it happens — so you always know your exact financial position.

The best expense tracker apps go beyond simple lists. They show you visual summaries (like charts and dashboards), track multiple bank accounts and wallets in one place, and update your net worth in real time every time a transaction is added.

Why do you need an expense tracker in 2026?

With UPI, credit cards, and digital wallets all coexisting, money moves faster than ever. Without a dedicated tracker, it is nearly impossible to understand your spending patterns, prepare for large purchases, or build genuine savings. An expense tracker removes the guesswork and puts you back in control.

  • See exactly where money goes — rent, groceries, subscriptions, dining
  • Know your net worth at a glance — total assets minus liabilities
  • Spot overspending early — the 7-day chart shows trends before they become problems
  • Plan large purchases — knowing your balance makes financial decisions easier
  • Build accountability — logging each transaction makes you more mindful of spending

FAQ

Questions about the expense tracker

Is the expense tracker really free?

Yes — 100% free with no limits. Sign in with Google, add your accounts, and start tracking. There are no limits on the number of accounts or transactions, and no subscription is required.

Which currencies are supported?

Currently INR (₹), USD ($), EUR (€), and GBP (£). Each account can have its own currency. More currencies will be added based on user demand.

Is my financial data secure?

Yes. Your data lives in Firebase Firestore under your own Google account. Firestore security rules ensure only your authenticated account can read or write your financial data. Not even we can access it.

Can I track multiple bank accounts?

Yes — add as many asset accounts as you need. Each tracks its own balance independently. Transfers between accounts are also supported and automatically update both balances.

What is the difference between a deposit, withdrawal, and transfer?

A deposit is money coming in (salary, freelance payment). A withdrawal is money going out (rent, groceries, bills). A transfer moves money between two of your own accounts (e.g., savings to current account) without affecting your net total.

Does it work on mobile?

Fully responsive. The dashboard, account cards, transaction list, and add-transaction modal all adapt to any screen. Adding a transaction on your phone takes under 10 seconds.

Can I use it without a Google account?

Yes — Guest mode gives you core access without login. Your data stays local. Sign in with Google to enable real-time Firestore sync across devices.
